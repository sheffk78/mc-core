## 3. BRAND COPY — Local Texture (Shelbi's Voice)

Hendersonville sits on the northern edge of Old Hickory Lake, and that lake shapes everything — the summer boat traffic, the weekend fishermen, the way the whole town seems to relax when the sun hits the water. It's one of Nashville's fastest-growing suburbs, but it still feels like its own place, not just a bedroom community with a zip code. You've got the lake on one side, farmland turning into subdivisions on the other, and about 20 minutes between you and downtown Nashville if traffic cooperates (which, listen, it doesn't always).

Sumner County has been one of the fastest-growing counties in Tennessee for the past decade, and you can feel it — new neighborhoods, new schools, young families everywhere. Drakes Creek Park is where the stroller set hangs out on Saturday mornings, and the greenway system connects more of the town every year.

TriStar Hendersonville is the hospital in town, and it's got the only NICU in all of Sumner County. That matters — it means you don't have to drive into Nashville for most birth scenarios. But if you're high-risk or need a Level IV NICU, Vanderbilt's Monroe Carell Children's Hospital is about 25 minutes south in Nashville, and they're the heavy hitters for complex neonatal cases in the entire region.

Weather-wise, Hendersonville gets the full Middle Tennessee experience. Summers are hot and humid (think 90°+ with thick air), spring is gorgeous with dogwoods and redbuds everywhere, fall is stunning along the lake, and winter is mild but unpredictable — ice storms can shut things down for a day or two. If you're due in January or February, have a backup plan for getting to the hospital.

Old Hickory Lake is the town's anchor — boating, fishing, lakeside dining, and those sunset views that make you forget you're 20 minutes from a major metro. Indian Lake Peninsula has restaurants and walking paths, and the annual Hendersonville Freedom Run on the Fourth of July is half community event, half family reunion.

Here's something important for Tennessee families: TennCare (that's our Medicaid program) does NOT currently cover doula services. That's different from Texas, different from Colorado, different from North Carolina. If you're on TennCare and want doula support, ask about sliding-scale options, community doula programs, or whether your HSA/FSA can help. Don't let the cost keep you from reaching out — many doulas in the area work with families on payment plans.
