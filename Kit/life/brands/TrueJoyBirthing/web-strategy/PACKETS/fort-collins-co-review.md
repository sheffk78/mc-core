## Medicaid/Doula Paragraph

> Colorado's Medicaid program, Health First Colorado, began covering doula services in July 2024 for qualifying pregnancies. In Fort Collins, some doulas are now billing Medicaid directly, while others still work privately with HSA/FSA funds. This is a real coverage benefit — not a pilot program, not a maybe-someday. If you're on Health First Colorado, ask your doula if they're enrolled as a Medicaid provider, or contact your managed care plan to find participating doulas.

## Hospital/NICU Paragraphs

### UCHealth Poudre Valley Hospital

> Poudre Valley Hospital, on Lemay Avenue in central-east Fort Collins, has been part of this community since 1925 — first as Larimer County Hospital, then PVH. It's where your neighbor's mom might have been born, and now you're birthing there too. That continuity matters, and so does their **verified Level III NICU** on Wikipedia, which is the safety net you hope you never need but are grateful exists. Doulas are generally welcome, though visitor policies shift, so confirm during your hospital tour. If you're delivering at PVH, having your birth plan ready makes intake smoother — they see a lot of families and move fast.

### UCHealth Medical Center of the Rockies

> UCHealth Medical Center of the Rockies, in Loveland about 15 minutes south, is the sister hospital to PVH in the UCHealth system — but for birthing, Fort Collins families go to PVH. MCR is the regional cardiac and neuroscience center, a Level I trauma hospital handling the heavy hitting in those areas. **Birthing services, if any, are limited. Contact MCR directly if you have questions about their maternity services.** If we're being real, Poudre Valley is your birthing hospital in Fort Collins — MCR is there if you need cardiac or trauma care, not labor and delivery.
