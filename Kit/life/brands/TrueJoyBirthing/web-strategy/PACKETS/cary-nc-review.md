## Medicaid/Doula Paragraph

> In North Carolina, Medicaid does cover doula services for eligible pregnancies through the state's Medicaid program. Not every doula is enrolled with Medicaid yet, so families in Cary often either work with a Medicaid-enrolled doula or pay privately and ask about HSA/FSA reimbursement. Contact your Medicaid managed care plan (Carolina Complete Health, UnitedHealthcare Community Plan, WellCare, or others) to confirm current doula benefits and participating providers.

## Hospital/NICU Paragraphs

### WakeMed Cary Hospital

> WakeMed Cary Hospital, off Kildaire Farm Road in western Cary, is the only full-service hospital in town — and if you're planning a straightforward delivery, it's a genuinely nice option. Their Women's Pavilion & Birthplace has a more personal, community-hospital feel compared to the bigger Raleigh campus. They've even got a Mother's Milk Bank on site, which not every hospital can say. **Here's the thing, though — WakeMed Cary does not have a NICU.** If anything goes sideways with your baby, they transfer to WakeMed Raleigh Campus, which has the only Level IV NICU in Wake County. So if you're high-risk, you'll want to plan to deliver at Raleigh Campus or one of the other hospitals with a higher-level NICU from the start rather than transfer mid-labor.

### UNC Rex Hospital

> UNC Rex Hospital, about 15 minutes from downtown Cary over on Lake Boone Trail in Raleigh, is where a lot of Cary families choose to deliver — it's close, it's part of the UNC Health system, and it has a strong reputation for women's services. They've got a Special Care Nursery for babies who need a little extra help, but for anything serious, you'd transfer to UNC Medical Center in Chapel Hill or WakeMed Raleigh.

### WakeMed Raleigh Campus

> WakeMed Raleigh Campus is about 18 minutes from Cary on New Bern Avenue, and it's where you go when things are complicated — they've got the only **Level IV NICU in Wake County** and the only Level I Trauma Center in the county too. They handle high-risk pregnancies, maternal-fetal medicine, and the cases that need the highest-level neonatal care. Also worth knowing: Duke University Hospital in Durham has a Level IV NICU too, about 25 minutes from Cary, and UNC Medical Center in Chapel Hill is about 30 minutes.
