Hendersonville families deserve birth-prep tools that know their hospital, their costs, and their options — from TriStar Hendersonville's community NICU to Vanderbilt's Level IV capability 25 minutes south. ## Medicaid/Doula Paragraph

> Right now, TennCare — that's Tennessee's Medicaid program — does not cover doula services. So families in Hendersonville are usually paying out of pocket or using HSA/FSA funds if they have them. That's one reason it helps to be very clear up front with your doula about fees and what's included. Some doulas offer sliding-scale packages or payment plans, and HSA/FSA reimbursement is an option with a letter from your provider. Don't let the cost keep you from asking — many doulas in the area work with families on this.

## Hospital/NICU Paragraphs

### TriStar Hendersonville Medical Center

> TriStar Hendersonville Medical Center, on New Shackle Island Road right in town, is the only hospital in Sumner County — and the only one with a NICU. Their birth center has spacious labor, delivery, and recovery suites and a lactation boutique for inpatient and outpatient support. **TriStar Hendersonville has a NICU for babies who need extra support, but higher-level cases are often transferred to Nashville.** It's your in-town option for a community hospital birth, and if you're low-risk, it's a solid choice.

### Vanderbilt University Medical Center

> Vanderbilt, about 25 minutes south in Nashville, is the heavy hitter for the whole region — **verified Level IV NICU** at Monroe Carell Jr. Children's Hospital, Maternal-Fetal Medicine specialists for high-risk pregnancies, and the go-to for the most complex neonatal cases in Middle Tennessee. If you know going in that your pregnancy is high-risk, Vanderbilt is worth the drive. They accept TennCare and most private insurance, and they've seen everything — which is exactly what you want when things are complicated.

## Remaining Uncertainties

We use soft language: "has a NICU for babies who need extra support, but higher-level cases are often transferred to Nashville." We do NOT hard-label the NICU level. This needs secondary verification before publish — could be confirmed with a phone call to TriStar Hendersonville's maternity department.

No verified freestanding birth center exists in Hendersonville, Sumner County, or Nashville metro. We state this clearly and note that Tennessee does not license CPMs, which limits out-of-hospital birth options.
3. **TennCare doula coverage** — Confirmed as not covering doula services as of research date (May 2026). Legislative changes could alter this; we note "Right now" framing but don't promise future changes.
