## 1. VERIFIED FACTS

### Hospitals

**WakeMed Cary Hospital**
- Address: 1900 Kildaire Farm Rd, Cary, NC 27511
- Neighborhood: Western Cary, just off US-1/64
- NICU: WakeMed Cary does NOT have a NICU. WakeMed's Level IV NICU is at the Raleigh Campus. Verified on en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WakeMed and wakemed.org.
- Known for: The only full-service hospital in Cary. Women's Pavilion & Birthplace, Mother's Milk Bank, 208 beds, Level III Trauma Center, Joint Commission accredited. More community-hospital vibe than the big Raleigh campus.
- Medicaid: Accepts Medicaid (WakeMed is nonprofit)

**WakeMed Raleigh Campus** (Raleigh, ~18 min from downtown Cary)
- Address: 3000 New Bern Ave, Raleigh, NC 27610
- Neighborhood: East Raleigh
- NICU: Verified Level IV NICU (en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WakeMed). The highest-level NICU in Wake County. Only Level I Trauma Center in Wake County.
- Known for: Maternal-Fetal Medicine specialists, high-risk pregnancy care, PICU. This is where Cary families with complex pregnancies go.
- Medicaid: Accepts Medicaid

**UNC Rex Hospital** (Raleigh, ~15 min from downtown Cary)
- Address: 4420 Lake Boone Trail, Raleigh, NC 27607
- Neighborhood: West Raleigh, very close to Cary border
- NICU: Special Care Nursery (estimated Level II equivalent). UNC Rex does NOT have a Level III or IV NICU — higher-level care transfers to UNC Medical Center in Chapel Hill (~30 min). Verified on UNC Rex Wikipedia page (general hospital, 665 beds).
- Known for: Well-regarded women's services, popular with Cary/Raleigh families. Part of UNC Health system. More personalized birthing experience than the Chapel Hill academic center.
- Medicaid: Accepts Medicaid

### Doula Cost Range
- Birth doula: **$800–$2,500** (Research Triangle market, affluent area skews higher)
- Postpartum doula: **$25–$40/hour**
- Not independently source-verified from Cary-specific pricing

### Insurance / HSA
- Major insurance: Blue Cross Blue Shield of NC, UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, Cigna
- HSA/FSA: May qualify with letter of medical necessity
- Most plans do NOT routinely cover doula services

### Medicaid — North Carolina
- **NC Medicaid covers doula services** as of 2024 (NCDHHS expansion). This is a **different framework** from Texas SB 750 — NC has its own state-specific Medicaid doula benefit.
- NC Medicaid managed care plans: Carolina Complete Health, UnitedHealthcare Community Plan, WellCare, etc.
- Enrollees should verify current participating doula networks and reimbursement through their specific Medicaid managed care plan
- Postpartum Medicaid coverage extends 12 months
- All major hospitals in Wake County accept Medicaid for delivery services

## 3. BRAND COPY — Local Texture in Shelbi's Voice

Cary is obsessed with green space in the best way — over 30 public parks and greenways, and Hemlock Bluffs Nature Preserve feels like stumbling into a mountain cove 20 minutes from downtown Raleigh. Fred G. Bond Metro Park is where half the town pushes strollers on Saturday mornings. If you're nesting and need to walk this baby out, the Cary Greenway system connects miles of shaded trails without you ever having to get on a road.

Okay, real talk — Cary is basically Raleigh's fancy sibling. The town line is porous. You can be in downtown Raleigh in 15 minutes and in Chapel Hill in 30, but I-40 and US-1/64 traffic during rush hour? She's a whole situation. Especially around the 540 loop and NC-55 construction. If you're laboring in the morning or evening rush, leave early for the hospital.

Summers here are brutal — think 95°F with 90% humidity and no breeze. If you're due July–September, hydrate like it's your job. Spring and fall are absolutely gorgeous — azaleas at the SAS campus, dogwoods everywhere. Winters are mild but we get ice storms that shut everything down.

Cary does everything well — the Downtown Farmers Market, Koka Booth Amphitheatre concerts, the Diwali Festival (one of the largest in the Southeast), Lazy Daze Arts & Crafts Festival. The Indian and Asian food scene is genuinely excellent. One of the most diverse towns in NC and it shows — in the food, the festivals, the community.

WakeMed Cary is the only hospital in town, and for straightforward deliveries it's a genuinely lovely option. The Women's Pavilion is known for that community-hospital feel. But here's the thing — they don't have a NICU. If anything goes sideways, they transfer to WakeMed Raleigh Campus, which has the only Level IV NICU in Wake County. So if you're high-risk, you'll want to deliver at Raleigh Campus or one of the other Level III/IV hospitals from the start rather than transfer mid-labor.
