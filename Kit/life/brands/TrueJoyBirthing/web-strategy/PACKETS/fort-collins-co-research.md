- **Colorado Medicaid covers doula services** effective July 1, 2024, as a Health First Colorado benefit

## 3. BRAND COPY — Local Texture (Shelbi's Voice)

Fort Collins sits where the eastern plains lean up against the Front Range, and you can see the mountains from just about any stretch of road in town. The Cache la Poudre River cuts through the northern edge — named for the gunpowder that French fur traders stashed along its banks — and Horsetooth Reservoir anchors the western skyline, that distinctive rock formation watching over everything like an old friend who always shows up.

This is a college town (Colorado State University, go Rams) that also happens to be one of Colorado's best-kept secrets for young families. The cost of living hasn't climbed the way it has in Boulder or Denver — not yet, anyway — and people notice.

Spring in Fort Collins means wind — real wind, the kind that makes you reconsider that patio plan. Summers are dry and golden, temps can spike past 95° but almost always cool down by evening thanks to that mountain breeze. Fall is the payoff: cottonwoods along the Poudre turning gold, Old Town's brick sidewalks covered in leaves. Winters are milder than you'd expect for a mountain-adjacent town — snow comes and goes, rarely sticking around long enough to feel oppressive.

If you're driving to Poudre Valley Hospital, know that College Avenue (Hwy 287) and Lemay Avenue are your main north-south corridors, and both can bottleneck during CSU semester rush hours. Leave extra time around 8am and 5pm. Harmony Road is the major east-west connector — use it to cross town, but budget for construction seasons, because Fort Collins is always working on Harmony.

Old Town Fort Collins is the heart of the community — brick buildings, independent bookstores, coffee roasters, and more craft breweries per capita than should be legal. Saturday mornings, the Larimer County Farmers Market sets up in Old Town. If you're nesting, this is where you'll want to wander — fresh flowers, local honey, and the kind of slow Saturday that makes the third trimester feel a little less heavy.
